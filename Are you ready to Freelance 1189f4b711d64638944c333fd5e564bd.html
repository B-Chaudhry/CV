Insights:

  1. List of People:
    • The distribution shows that 'Somewhat clear' has a higher count across most categories compared to 'Not clear'.
    • This suggests that individuals who are 'Somewhat clear' about their ideal client tend to have a more defined list of people they know.
  1. Public Profile:
    • The distribution indicates that 'Somewhat clear' individuals are more likely to have an active and consistent public profile compared to 'Not clear' individuals.
    • 'Not clear' individuals are more likely to have been quiet for some time.
  1. Support Network:
    • The distribution shows that 'Somewhat clear' individuals tend to have a stronger support network compared to 'Not clear' individuals.
    • 'Not clear' individuals are more likely to have some support but could be stronger.
  1. Time Available:
    • The distribution indicates that there is no significant difference in the time available between 'Not clear' and 'Somewhat clear' individuals.
    • Both groups have similar distributions across different categories of time availability.

In summary, individuals who are 'Somewhat clear' about their ideal client tend to have a more defined list of people, a more active public profile, and a stronger support network compared to those who are 'Not clear'. However, the time available does not show a significant difference between the two groups.

How much time people want to spend on their business ?

  • How the Counts Are Distributed on the Graphs (just for your understanding):
    1. Y-axis (Count):
      • The y-axis represents the number of respondents in each category. For instance, if there are 5 respondents with "1-4 years" of experience who have "Intermediate" clarity of offering, the height of the corresponding bar segment will be 5.
    1. X-axis (Years of Experience):
      • The x-axis categorizes respondents based on their years of experience. Each bar corresponds to a specific range of years (e.g., "Less than 1 year", "1-4 years", "5-9 years", "10+ years").
    1. Bar Segments:
      • Each bar is divided into segments representing the different levels of 'Clarity_of_offering' or 'Ideal_client_'. The segments are stacked on top of each other.
      • For 'Clarity_of_offering', we have segments like "Very Clear", "Clear", "Somewhat Clear", "Not Clear".
      • For 'Ideal_client_', you might have segments like "Very Clear", "Clear", "Somewhat Clear", "Not Clear".

Do individuals with more knowledge maintain a public profile?

There is a moderate positive correlation between having knowledge in the climate space and maintaining a public profile. This suggests that individuals who are knowledgeable in this specific area are likely to engage more publicly, possibly through presentations, publications, or online content related to climate issues.


Application: Motivate knowledgeable freelancers to engage publicly through blogs, webinars, or social media. This can enhance their visibility and credibility, benefiting both your business and their careers.

Do individuals with more knowledge have a support network?

Not really, the correlation between knowledge in the climate space and having a support network is relatively low. This indicates that while expertise in a niche area like climate might enhance public engagement, it does not necessarily translate into a stronger or more supportive network.

Application: Facilitate networking opportunities for your freelancers by organizing events, webinars, and online communities. Encourage them to build public profiles to strengthen their support networks. This is something IMMA is already doing.

Do people with more knowledge have knowledge on ideal clients?

No, the correlation between knowledge in the climate space and clarity about the ideal client is also low. This could imply that while individuals may have specialized knowledge, they might not have clearly defined their target clientele who could benefit from this expertise.

Application: IMMA should concentrate on instructing knowledgeable individuals on how to identify potential clients, as this is an area where many people lack expertise.

What types of people have support networks?

There appears to be a strong correlation between having a public profile and having a strong support network. This indicates that individuals who are more visible and active publicly tend to have better support networks, which could be crucial for freelancing success.
